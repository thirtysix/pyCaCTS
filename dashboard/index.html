pyCaCTS

Master transcription factors across the DepMap cell-line panel

A fast, validated Python reimplementation of CaCTS (Cancer Core Transcription factor Specificity): it scores how lineage-specifically each transcription factor is expressed and reads out the candidate master regulators of any cancer group. It runs on two panels:

DepMap / CCLE: ~1,450 cell lines, grouped at every Oncotree level (lineage, disease, subtype, model type) and each line on its own.
TCGA: tumors by tumor type, molecular subtype, and sample type, so you can compare a tumor with its cell-line models.

Scores match the original R to floating-point precision, computed thousands of times faster (see below).

MTF atlas

Master transcription factors for every group. The Data toggle switches between DepMap cell lines (five resolutions, down to the single cell line) and TCGA tumors (tumor type, molecular subtype, sample type). Each group's TFs split into two calls:

Specific: empirical-null FDR < 0.10 and mean ≥ 1 TPM, significantly group-specific and expressed.
Non-specific: top-5% expressed but not FDR-significant, CaCTS's candidate ubiquitous (multi-cancer) MTFs.

Tumor vs model

Tumor-only: lost in culture, often microenvironment / differentiation TFs.
Model-only: gained in culture, not seen in the tumor.

Within-cancer master TFs

Inside a single cancer, which master TFs distinguish its subgroups. CaCTS is re-scored on that cancer's own samples, split by the Axis toggle: molecular subtype, tumor vs adjacent-normal, or AJCC stage (I to IV). The Call toggle picks how a master TF is defined:

Significant: empirical-null FDR < 0.10 and mean ≥ 1 TPM (the tool-wide call). Favours the most subgroup-discriminative factors: breast LumA surfaces the progesterone receptor PGR.
Abundant: the top-5% expressed TFs ranked by specificity, the highly-expressed canonical masters. Breast LumA / LumB → ESR1 / FOXA1 / GATA3.

Within one shared lineage the two views diverge and complement each other; some subgroups have many master TFs, some none, which is itself informative.
